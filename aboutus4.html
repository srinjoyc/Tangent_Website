What is Tangent Collective?

We set out to bridge the gap between the technical and non-technical communities.

Great ideas come to a lot of people, but few act upon it due to the complexities of seeing it through. We think that we are able to bring these ideas to fruition in an affordable and timely manner through understanding, foresight, and planning.

We started this concept in university but the sheer demand of our service encouraged us to turn this into a real business - so we did. Since then, we've grown our abilities and gained some experience under our belt and we hope to continue to do so by serving our communities through what we have learnt.

Our end goal is to have a fluid channel of designers, product managers, developers, and captalists all connect and come together as a collective to bring the best ideas to life.

We also wanted to create a space for developers to solicit projects of all shapes and sizes. Our team members are devs by trade and we have a consistent urge to build great ideas but finding them and getting paid for them can be a difficult task. Even if you do find a project you can get passionate about, there are often a lot of logistical and financial obstacles that often make you reconsider. That's where we want to come in and aid in the parts that devs don't like to deal with, and by doing so we believe it fosters an environment where both parties have the best chance of success.

We enjoy creating a balance of business and tech, and making them work in harmony is what we strive to do. We have a passion for finding the best solutions on how to do something, we leave the 'what' to you.

Agency rates typically vary between $80-$200/hr.

They have incentive to sell you on the most expensive options and get their billable hours as high as possible. Most software projects will rarely require that amount of experience and building all the functionalities from scratch, but it's in their best interest to do it the hardest way possible and sell you on the years of experience their developers have - if you need it or not.

The risk is on you.

Getting a freelancer that is a good fit for your team, passionate about your project, and possesses the right skills can be difficult and daunting task. Once hired, their management, performance and engagement is highly variable. Hiring a lot of them gets even more complicated and team chemistry comes into play. Instead of consistently worrying about their work and if you have made the right decision, consider Tangent - the concept of managed freelancing.

Extreme variablity, communication issues, and uncertaintity.

It's too often that outsourcing goes wrong. We've seen first hand how reputated companies get into outsourcing core parts of their project and end up with un-maintainable code that others refuse to work on. Outsourcing can be great given you outsource the correct parts and have native speakers and constant safegaurds and oversight prior to accepting work.
